Chemical Reactions and Equations


  • What is a Chemical Reaction?

    • A chemical reaction is a process where one or more substances, called reactants, are converted into one or more different substances, called products.










    Types of Chemical Reactions

    • Combination Reaction
    • Decomposition Reaction
    • Displacement Reaction
    • Double Displacement Reaction
    • Redox Reaction






    Types of Chemical Equations



      • There are two types of chemical equations

    1. Unbalanced / Skeletal Chemical Equation
    2. Balanced Chemical Equation











    Unbalanced Chemical Equation

    An unbalanced chemical equation is a chemical equation where the number of atoms of each element on the reactant side is not equal to the number of atoms of each element on the product side.

    Example:
    H2 (g) Hydrogen + O2 (g) Oxygen → H2O (l) Water is an unbalanced equation.


    Explanation:
    The number of atoms of hydrogen and oxygen on the reactant side is not equal to the number of atoms of hydrogen and oxygen on the product side.









    Balanced Chemical Equation

    A balanced chemical equation is a chemical reaction equation that has the same number of atoms of each element and the same total charge on both sides of the equation. This means that the mass and charge are balanced on both sides of the reaction.

    For example, the equation 2Mg + O2 → 2MgO is balanced because it has the same number of atoms of magnesium and oxygen on both sides.
